What Affects the Price of Carpentry Work?

Materials

The timber species you choose has the biggest impact on cost. Softwood (redwood pine) is the most affordable option for structural work and painted joinery. Hardwoods like oak, ash, and iroko cost 2-4 times more but offer superior durability and aesthetics. For flooring, engineered oak boards are typically 15-20% cheaper than solid oak while offering better stability over underfloor heating.

Complexity and Design

A straight-flight staircase in softwood might cost around £2,000, but add a quarter turn, oak treads, glass balustrade, and under-stair storage and you could be looking at £6,000-£8,000. Similarly, a standard panel door fitting is £150, but hanging a heavy solid oak door in a period property with an out-of-square frame requires significantly more skill and time.

Access and Location

Properties in remote rural locations across Cornwall and Devon can add 5-15% to project costs due to travel time and delivery logistics. Difficult access—narrow lanes, upper floors without lifts, or working within occupied conservation areas—also affects pricing. We always account for these factors in our initial quote so there are no surprises.

Property Age and Condition

Older Cornish cottages and Devon longhouses often have uneven floors, thick stone walls, and non-standard dimensions. This means more time spent scribing, adjusting, and working with the character of the building rather than against it. Heritage work requires specialist knowledge but delivers beautiful results that respect the property's history.

Why Costs Vary Between Tradespeople

You may receive quotes that differ significantly between carpenters. Cheaper quotes sometimes exclude materials, skip preparation work, or reflect less experience. When comparing quotes, check whether they include:

  • All materials, fixings, and finishing products
  • Waste removal and site clean-up
  • Making good to surrounding areas (plastering, painting touching up)
  • Public liability insurance and any guarantees

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the average day rate for a carpenter in Cornwall?

The average day rate for a qualified carpenter in Cornwall ranges from £200 to £320 per day in 2026, depending on the complexity of the work and the tradesperson's experience. Specialist joinery work such as bespoke staircases or heritage restoration typically commands higher rates of £280-£350 per day. Most reputable carpenters quote on a project basis rather than a day rate, which gives you cost certainty.
